## Interview Preparation Strategy

### Coding Rounds
- Follow: NeetCode 150 → Striver SDE Sheet → Company-specific lists
- Practice talking through your approach out loud
- Track time complexity and space complexity for every solution

### System Design Rounds
- Resources: Grokking System Design, ByteByteGo, Alex Xu's "System Design Interview" book
- Practice designing: Instagram, WhatsApp, URL shortener, ride-sharing app

### Behavioral Rounds
- Use STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Prepare 10 stories covering: leadership, conflict, failure, impact, collaboration
- Study Amazon Leadership Principles — asked widely across companies

### Mock Interviews
- Platforms: Pramp (free), Interviewing.io, peer mocks with classmates
- Start from Month 10, aim for 10+ sessions before placement season

## Key Observations & Learnings

1. **DSA is the bottleneck, not language knowledge.** Java is fine for interviews, but without consistent DSA practice, even strong programmers fail the screening round at product companies.

2. **15 hours/week is sufficient if structured.** Random grinding is less effective than following a curated sheet and tracking progress weekly.

3. **Projects multiply resume value.** A GitHub with 2–3 real, deployed projects signals far more than listed skills alone.

4. **AI interest is an asset, not a distraction.** Adding Python + ML after month 8 creates a second viable career track without compromising the primary goal.

5. **Timeline is a real advantage.** Starting preparation in 6th semester gives 12–18 months of runway before campus placements — enough time to cover DSA thoroughly, build projects, and crack internships.

6. **System design is often overlooked by students.** Even for SDE-1 roles, many companies include a basic system design round. Starting it at Month 7 gives adequate preparation time.

7. **Behavioral interviews matter more than expected.** Companies like Amazon weight behavioral rounds heavily. Preparing 10 STAR stories early prevents last-minute cramming.
